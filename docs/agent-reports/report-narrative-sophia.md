## 1. Critical Concerns & Cross-Agent Responses

### Concern A: The "Tonal Whiplash" of The Novice

**The Issue:** _The Novice_ (WarioWare) is slapstick comedy. It comes right after _The Protector_ (Miscarriage). Even with the "Time Jump," the sudden shift to "Wacky Minigames" might feel disrespectful or jarring to the player's grief.

- **Risk Level:** High (Narrative Dissonance)

**Responses:**

- **Samus (Design):** "We can start _The Novice_ slow and somber. The first few micro-games are 'Take your pills' (quiet). Then, as the hormones kick in, the speed ramps up. The _chaos_ becomes the metaphor for the IVF drugs, not just 'wackiness' for its own sake."
- **Glados (Arch):** "I can use the Color Grading to bridge this. Start _The Novice_ with the desaturated palette of _The Protector_, and slowly bleed the saturation in as the chaos increases."

### Concern B: The Ending Payoff

**The Issue:** The game ends with _The Mourner_ (Unpacking) and then _The Arrival_. If the "Baby" reveal feels generic, the whole journey falls flat. The player needs to feel a specific connection, not just "Oh look, a baby model."

- **Risk Level:** High (Emotional Payoff)

**Responses:**

- **Samus (Design):** "We bring back the mechanics. In the final scene, let the player use the _Tuner_ mechanic to hear the cry, the _Partner_ mechanic to hold the hand. Call back to the skills they learned."
- **Gordon (Dev):** "I will spend extra budget on the Baby shader. It needs Subsurface Scattering and 'Peach Fuzz.' It cannot look like a plastic doll. It needs to look fragile."
